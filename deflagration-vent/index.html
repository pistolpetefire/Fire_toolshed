Deflagration Vent Sizing — Help / Input Guide

This tool sizes deflagration vents so explosion pressure is limited to a reduced value (Pred) the room or equipment can survive. Primary basis: NFPA 68 (Explosion Protection by Deflagration Venting). IBC / IFC often require explosion control and reference NFPA 68 / 69 / 652.

Most common use: room vent sizing. Pick Room — combustible dust (or room gas), enter L×W×H and a construction preset, then KSt / KG. Process-equipment paths are for vessels, collectors, bins, and silos.

Quick start — room (dust)

  1. Choose Room — combustible dust.
  2. Set units (US ft/psi is default for rooms).
  3. Enter room length, width, height (interior clear).
  4. Pick construction (sets starting Pstat, Pred, allowable pressure).
  5. Enter KSt from dust testing; adjust Pstat/Pred if needed.
  6. Optional: available wall/roof vent area and panel size.
  7. Read required free area Av and geometric area (after efficiency). Print/Save for the package.

1 · Application paths (start screen)

Room — combustible dust (most common)
Buildings and interior rooms with combustible dust (packaging, milling, recycling, wood, food, etc.). Geometry is from L×W×H; volume and L/D are calculated. Construction presets seed room-strength pressures. Standards context: NFPA 68 + dust hazard analysis (NFPA 652/654); IBC/IFC may require explosion control.
Room — gas / vapor
Same room geometry workflow, but fuel is flammable gas/vapor using KG. Confirm concentration, ignition scenario, and NFPA 68 gas chapter applicability.
Process equipment — dust
Baghouses, bins, silos, mills, vessels. Enter free volume and L/D (or L and D). For dust collectors use dirty-side volume that can participate in the deflagration.
Process equipment — gas / vapor
Process vessels with gas/vapor hazard (KG). Compact gas correlation; elongated cases need full NFPA 68 treatment.

3 · Room geometry (room paths only)

Room length / width / height
Interior clear dimensions of the vented room. Geometric volume V = L × W × H. Floor area = L × W (shown in results).
How L/D is calculated for rooms
  • L = longest of L, W, H (longest flame-path dimension).
  • Cross-section area A = V ÷ L.
  • Deq = 2 × √(A / π) (equivalent diameter of that cross-section).
  • L/D = L ÷ Deq.
  • L/D ≤ 2 → compact dust formula.
  • 2 < L/D ≤ 6 → elongated multiplier (more vent area).
  • L/D > 6 → outside simple method; tool warns — use full NFPA 68 / specialist analysis.
Calculated room volume / L/D (read-only)
Live display of geometric V (and V_eff if fill < 1) and L/D with L and Deq for checking inputs.
Deflagrable fill fraction
Fraction of room volume used as V_eff in the vent equation (default 1.0 = full room). Values below 1.0 imply a partial-volume dust/gas cloud. Only reduce with documented justification (NFPA 68 partial-volume methods). Lower fill reduces calculated vent area — do not reduce casually.

4 · Room construction & vent placement (room paths only)

Building / room construction
Preset starting points for pressures (US psi g). Switching a preset overwrites Pstat, Pred, and allowable pressure (unless you use Custom and type values yourself). Always verify with a structural engineer.
  • Light metal / panel — Pstat ≈ 0.5 psi, Pred ≈ 1.5 psi, allowable ≈ 2.5 psi. Weak rooms → large vents.
  • Masonry / tilt-up — ≈ 0.75 / 2.5 / 4.0 psi.
  • Reinforced concrete — ≈ 1.0 / 3.5 / 6.0 psi. Higher strength may allow higher Pred (smaller vents).
  • Custom — enter all pressures manually; preset does not override.
Primary vent location
Exterior wall(s), roof, or both. Affects design notes and discharge planning only (not a separate formula). Discharge must not endanger people, exits, or adjacent buildings (fireball / blast / flame jet).
Available wall/roof vent area (optional)
How much exterior wall or roof area can actually be used for blow-out panels or lightweight cladding. The app compares this to required geometric area and flags if the available area is short.

5 · Process equipment geometry (process paths only)

Enclosure volume V
Free volume of the protected equipment. Dust collectors: dirty-side volume that can explode (not necessarily clean-air volume).
L/D
Shape factor for vessels. ≤2 compact; 2–6 elongated multiplier; >6 special methods required.
Length L / hydraulic diameter D
Optional. If both are entered, L/D is set automatically as L ÷ D. For non-circular sections, D is often hydraulic diameter 4A/P.

6 · Deflagration & vent parameters

KSt (dust paths)
Dust deflagration index (bar·m/s) from closed-vessel testing (e.g. ASTM E1226 / ISO 6184). Higher KSt → more violent explosion → more vent area. Classes shown for reference: St 1 ≤ 200, St 2 ≤ 300, St 3 > 300. Prefer project-specific test data.
KG (gas paths)
Gas/vapor deflagration index (bar·m/s). Depends on composition and concentration. Order-of-magnitude examples only (methane ≈ 55, propane ≈ 100, hydrogen ≈ 550) — do not design from examples alone.
Pstat — vent static activation
Gauge pressure at which the vent panel opens (manufacturer rating). Room panels often about 0.5–1.5 psi g. Higher Pstat can increase required vent area.
Pred — reduced explosion pressure
Maximum pressure allowed in the room/equipment during a vented deflagration. Lower Pred (weak rooms) requires more vent area. Must be greater than Pstat and less than structural allowable pressure.
Allowable enclosure pressure (optional)
Structural limit from engineering assessment. Tool errors if Pred exceeds this; warns if Pred is above about ⅔ of this value (common NFPA 68 discussion of strength limits — confirm for the project).
Vent efficiency Ef
Fraction of geometric opening that acts as free vent (0.1–1.0). 1.0 = ideal free vent. Hinged panels, frames, screens, and covers reduce Ef. Geometric area ≈ Av ÷ Ef. Ducted vents need additional NFPA 68 corrections (not fully modeled).
Panel free area each (optional)
If one panel’s free area is known, estimates how many panels are needed (ceil of geometric area ÷ panel size).
Notes
Fuel, DHA reference, occupancy, outdoor discharge path, adjacent exposures — printed on the report.

7 · Results explained

Required free vent area Av
Calculated effective vent area from the NFPA 68-style equation for the path and L/D regime.
Geometric area (with efficiency)
Physical opening area to install: Av ÷ Ef. What you typically specify for panels or blow-out wall area.
Room L/D · regime
Shape regime: compact vs elongated (and room vs process labeling).
Wall/roof area check
If available vent area was entered, shows whether it meets the geometric requirement.
Checks — OK / CHECK / ERROR
OK — calculation completed without hard errors (still use engineering judgment). CHECK — completed with warnings (envelope, fill, strength, wall area, etc.). ERROR — invalid inputs (e.g. missing dimensions, Pred ≤ Pstat, Pred above strength).

9 · Standards (what this is / is not)

  • NFPA 68 — primary standard for deflagration vent sizing (rooms and equipment).
  • NFPA 69 — explosion prevention, isolation, suppression (not sized here).
  • NFPA 652 / 654 — dust hazard analysis; may require room or equipment protection.
  • IBC / IFC — building/fire codes that can require explosion control by reference to NFPA methods.

Not a substitute for: dust hazard analysis (DHA), structural PE sign-off on allowable pressure, listed vent assembly design, full ducted-vent or panel-inertia methods, hybrid dust–gas mixtures, or consequence analysis of the outdoor fireball/blast. Confirm the adopted NFPA 68 edition and AHJ requirements.
